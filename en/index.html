Ordinary Japanese Dictionary is built for everyday reading and study. Detailed entries with example sentences, proper names, kanji breakdowns, furigana and pitch accent — and all of it works offline. Inspired by jisho.org.

About the dictionary 辞書について

There are plenty of electronic Japanese dictionaries.

But on a trip to Japan I kept missing very basic things:

  • OCR is often paid, and I was not ready to pay just for OCR;
  • dictionaries with Russian are often thin — probably because Warodai's license does not fit paid products well, or simply because the market is small and people rarely think about it;
  • many dictionaries try to keep you inside the app, while I wanted quick jumps to WaniKani, which I actively use for kanji;
  • I wanted an easy way to export lookup history, so the dictionary would not push its built-in flashcards instead of Anki;
  • I wanted more visual settings, at least enough to tune the interface a little;
  • many interfaces contain a lot of English and other unrelated labels, which distract from the Japanese.

Dictionaries with unique features and strong content — such as Monokakido or the built-in iOS dictionaries — can be forgiven a lot. But most dictionary apps are built on top of JMdict and mostly differ in the interface around the same data.

So I decided to make something that solved my own problem. I hope it can be useful to someone else too.

Obviously, not everyone in the world needs Russian. So I added custom dictionary import in the app's own format. For mobile dictionaries, this still seems fairly rare.

Custom dictionaries are a superset of jmdict-simplified. That means JMdict dictionaries can be imported as-is, although example sentences there are English-only. I hope the format description and modern LLMs will help people who need this.

Why not use the Yomitan format? Because it is too varied for this app.

Did I make everything exactly the way I wanted? No. I spent a lot of effort on layout and typography, but I am not a designer, and I fear it shows. OCR could also split text into sentences better.

The word counter in the reader is not always transparent to the user, and sometimes it would be better to hide it. Search for English and Russian words sometimes competes with romaji and Polivanov-system search. But to me these are small rough edges: trying to fix them right now may break more than it fixes. At least in the first version.
